Separation of Variables:

📅 13 April 2026 🏷️ Electromagnetism
"And "God" said, 'Let there be light,' and there were Maxwell's equations."

1. problem statement

An infinitely long rectangular metal pipe (sides \(a\) and \(b\)) is grounded, but one end, at \(x = 0\), is maintained at a specified potential \(\phi_0(y,z)\), as indicated in the figure below. Find the potential inside the pipe.

Applying the method of separation of variables in Cartesian coordinates to Laplace's equation \[ \nabla^2 \phi = 0 \] and given the zero boundary conditions on the four side walls, the general solution is a double Fourier series in sines with exponential decay in $x$: \[ \phi(x,y,z) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} A_{nm} \, e^{-\pi \sqrt{(n/a)^2 + (m/b)^2} \, x} \, \sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{a}\right) \sin\left(\frac{m\pi z}{b}\right). \] The coefficients $A_{nm}$ are determined from the boundary condition at $x=0$: \[ \phi_0(y,z) = \phi(0,y,z) = \sum_{n,m} A_{nm} \sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{a}\right) \sin\left(\frac{m\pi z}{b}\right). \] For the case of constant potential $\phi_0(y,z) = \phi_0$, the integral gives \[ A_{nm} = \frac{4 \phi_0}{ab} \int_0^a \sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{a}\right) dy \int_0^b \sin\left(\frac{m\pi z}{b}\right) dz. \] Evaluating the integral, we obtain that $A_{nm} = 0$ if $n$ or $m$ are even. For $n$ and $m$ odd: \[ A_{nm} = \frac{16 \phi_0}{\pi^2 n m} \] Therefore, the final solution is: \[ \phi(x,y,z) = \frac{16 \phi_0}{\pi^2} \sum_{\substack{n=1\\ n \text{ impar}}}^{\infty} \sum_{\substack{m=1\\ m \text{ impar}}}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n m} e^{-\pi \sqrt{(n/a)^2 + (m/b)^2} \, x} \sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{a}\right) \sin\left(\frac{m\pi z}{b}\right). \]
